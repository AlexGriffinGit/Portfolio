My Projects


We All Die Poor

We All Die Poor is a 2D wave survival game that was created during the 2018 Ukie Student Games Jam at Staffordshire University by me and 4 of my friends. The game jam theme that year was change, our team took this literally and we decided to make a mathematical based game based on shooting the correct amount of change (pounds and pence) to defeat enemies.

Learn more

Dwelling

Dwellings is a needs simulator revolving around surviving as someone who is living rough. Dwellings was conceived and created during the Global Game Jam 2019 at Staffordshire University by me and 4 of my friends. The theme for the game jam was What Home Means To You, our team took this in a different direction and focused on the hardships of people who are not so fortunate and are without a home.

Learn more

Chill Pill

Chill Pill is a physics based mobile arcade game developed in the Unity Game Engine. It was developed in my spare time during lockdown, I started development around the end of April and released it on the Google Play Store on the 17th of June. The game play is very simplistic and arcadey and was designed to be picked up and put down easily.

Crowd Simulation Android App

This Crowd Simulation Android App was developed by me for use in one of my university modules. It models basic AI crowd simulation algorithms running on an Android Device. It was created using the Unity Game Engine.

Box

Box is a 2D physics platformer created using the ODBAR Engine (See ODBAR Engine listing). Box has the player take control of a sentient box trying to escape an abandoned underwater research facility. The facility is full of hazards that the player has to carefully dodge and avoid.

ODBAR Engine

The ODBAR Engine is a game engine that was developed as part of a work simulation module during my time at Staffordshire University. The ODBAR Engine is primarily aimed at developing platformers, it includes many features that can be used for different types of platformers.

About Me

Hi I'm Alex, and as you might have learnt I've just graduated from Staffordshire University with a Computer Games Programming degree. Now I'm looking for the next step of my career.


I enjoy programming and writing code and love seeing my vision for projects start to take shape (in fact that's what I loved about making this portfolio), even if most of my projects are still plagued by programmer art, see project pages for examples.

Most of my time at university was geared towards the game side of programming. I thoroughly enjoy developing interesting ideas and mechanics for all types of games and I believe I can bring those interesting ideas and passion to a business environment.

I'm most comfortable using C++ and C# and like the experience that Unity provides but I'm open to all opportunities and would use any chance to expand my existing skillset.
I'm currently making a mobile game in Unity called Chill Pill, I'm looking at learning Javascript and Python as they're versatile and flexible.
